## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience: fleet/operations managers on desktop (primary) and drivers checking their own earnings and trips on mobile (secondary). Managers are the power users — they work with driver compliance, dispatching, compensation, and reporting during business hours. Drivers use it situationally to check their stats and receive communications.

### Brand Personality
Authoritative and professional. Serious, structured, trustworthy. This is a business-critical operations tool — it needs to project competence and reliability. Not cold or bureaucratic, but not casual either. Think enterprise software done well.

### Aesthetic Direction
Fleet management / logistics domain. Light mode is appropriate — used in office contexts during business hours. Dark mode support exists for driver preference flexibility. Blue accent (#1D4ED8) conveys authority and trust, appropriate for the domain.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — operators under pressure need instant comprehension, not decoration
2. **Status at a glance** — compliance, risk, and trip status must be scannable without reading
3. **Mobile as a real use case** — drivers checking their dashboard on phones is a first-class interaction, not an afterthought
4. **Earned trust** — the interface must feel reliable and precise, like the data it represents
5. **Minimal but not minimal-ist** — no unnecessary chrome, but information density matters in an ops tool
